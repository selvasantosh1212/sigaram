## Ratio & Proportion (Part B, not yet authored)
Source: 2025_q1 page 126 Q192 (real, with answer key mark).
- "A man spends Rs.500 in buying 12 tables and chairs. The cost of one table is Rs.50 and that of one chair is Rs.40. What is the ratio of the number of chairs and tables purchased?" → Answer: 5:1.
- Working: tables=t, chairs=c. t+c=12; 50t+40c=500. Solve: c=10, t=2. Ratio chairs:tables = 10:2 = 5:1.

## Volume (Part B, not yet authored — later topic)
Source: 2025_q1 page 79 Q140 (real, with answer key mark).
- "The diameter of a sphere is 8cm. It is melted and drawn into a wire of diameter 3mm. Find the length of the wire." → Answer: 37.9 m.
- Method: Volume is conserved when melting/recasting. Sphere volume (4/3)πr³ with r=4cm equals wire (cylinder) volume πr²h with r=0.15cm. Solve for h: h = (4/3)(4³)/(0.15²) ≈ 3792.6 cm ≈ 37.9 m.

## Area (Part B, not yet authored — later topic)
Source: 2024_q page 31 Q44 (real, with answer key mark).
- "The curved surface area of a right circular cylinder of height 7 cm is 66 cm². Find the diameter of the cylinder." → Answer: 3 cm.
- Method: CSA = 2πrh → 66 = 2×(22/7)×r×7 = 44r → r = 1.5 cm → diameter = 3 cm.

- "Choose correct statement(s) on VP qualifications: (i) must be a citizen of India (ii) must have completed 35 years of age (iii) must be qualified for election as a member of the Lok Sabha" → Answer: (i) and (ii) only. Classic trap: the real constitutional requirement (Article 66(3)) is qualification for election to the RAJYA SABHA, not the Lok Sabha — statement (iii) swaps the House deliberately.
